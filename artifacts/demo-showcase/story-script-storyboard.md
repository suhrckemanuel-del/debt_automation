# Story concept, script, and storyboard

## Concept: “Three numbers. One position.”

A reviewer is about to rely on a covenant number. The original agreement says
65%. An amendment says 70%. A narrow waiver mentions 72%. The tension is not
document search; it is deciding which statement controls, for which date, and
for which action. The product resolves the hierarchy, then gets out of the
reviewer's way by exposing exact evidence and the next human check.

## Full walkthrough script (target: 100 seconds)

**0:00–0:08 — Hook**

“The agreement says 65%. The amendment says 70%. The waiver mentions 72%.
Using the newest number would be an easy—and consequential—mistake.”

**0:08–0:20 — Decision surface**

“As of 2 July 2026, the amendment controls. The contractual threshold is 70%.
The 72% figure is limited relief for one Test Date; it does not amend the
threshold.”

**0:20–0:32 — Hidden second issue**

“And the waiver does something else: it separately blocks Distributions through
30 September. That changes the next action, even though it does not change the
covenant.”

**0:32–0:47 — Time and hierarchy**

“Move the review date and the controlling position changes. Before the
amendment, 65% controls. After the amendment expires, 65% resumes. Original
terms, amendments, and limited waivers stay distinct.”

**0:47–1:02 — Evidence**

“Every factual statement links to the persisted document title, clause or
section, page, and exact passage. The reviewer can inspect the waiver language
without trusting a summary.”

**1:02–1:18 — Ask workflow**

“A narrow question returns a saved, manifest-linked answer. The current
threshold is 70%, with the limited 72% relief called out separately.”

**1:18–1:31 — Safe failure**

“Ask for a covenant the sources do not contain and the system says ‘Source not
found.’ Ask whether a transaction is legally permitted and it refuses the final
conclusion, surfaces the relevant clauses, and requires human review.”

**1:31–1:40 — Close**

“This is a local synthetic v0 for evidence-backed first-pass review—not a
replacement for legal or commercial judgment. Would this make the review
clearer, and what would you still need to verify?”

## Teaser script (target: 40 seconds)

“65%. 70%. 72%. All three appear in the agreement set—but only one is the
current contractual threshold. The amendment controls at 70%. The waiver gives
limited relief to 72% for one Test Date and separately blocks Distributions.
Agreement Intelligence keeps those effects distinct, links every fact to the
exact passage, and abstains when support or legal judgment is missing. Local,
synthetic, and built for a human reviewer to verify.”

## Recording direction

- Capture at 1440×900; render in a 1920×1080 frame with a restrained browser
  surround.
- Use slow 2–4% pushes and short dissolves. No cursor choreography is required
  where the destination can be shown more legibly as a clean state change.
- Keep overlays to one sentence or one number at a time.
- Use the product's mint accent, amber only for limited-waiver attention, and no
  stock footage.
- Captions should be burned in and kept above the lower browser chrome.

## Voiceover plan

Preferred: one calm human voice, conversational pace around 145 words per
minute, close-mic, no “announcer” emphasis. Pause briefly after “72%?” and
before “it does not amend the threshold.”

Fallback: publish the rendered visual sequence with timed on-screen narration
and the full transcript beside it. Do not use a synthetic voice if it makes the
prototype feel more automated or authoritative than it is.
