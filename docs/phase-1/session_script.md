# Phase -1 Workflow-Review Session Script

**Target duration:** 45 minutes

**Required sessions:** 5

**Materials:** synthetic corpus, three-question reviewer pack, timer, evidence template

## Before the session

1. Assign a de-identified participant ID such as `P01`.
2. Confirm the participant is a target practitioner, reviewer, or both.
3. Do not collect employer, deal, borrower, lender, asset, or counterparty names.
4. Ask the participant not to share real documents or non-public terms.
5. Prepare separate links or files for:
   - the synthetic source documents; and
   - the completed reviewer pack.
6. Do not show the completed reviewer pack during the baseline exercise.

## 0–5 minutes — Opening and safety

Suggested wording:

> We are testing a workflow, not evaluating you. Everything shown is synthetic. Please describe real experiences only at a generic level and do not share names, documents, or non-public terms. I want candid evidence about whether this reduces or adds work.

Confirm:

- role category;
- whether the participant prepares, reviews, or does both;
- permission to take de-identified notes; and
- whether timing can be recorded.

## 5–12 minutes — Recent behaviour

Ask questions 1–5 from `outreach_questions.md`.

Required evidence:

- one recent comparable example;
- frequency or triggering event;
- current workaround;
- analyst burden;
- reviewer burden; and
- most important failure concern.

If the participant cannot name a recent comparable example, record that directly. Do not rescue the signal with a hypothetical.

## 12–23 minutes — Baseline workflow

Give the participant the three synthetic source documents and the three questions, but not the completed reviewer pack.

Ask:

> Please show or explain how you would establish an answer suitable for review. Think aloud, and stop when you would normally hand the work to another person.

Record:

- start and stop time;
- documents and clauses inspected;
- cross-references followed;
- whether the amendment and waiver are distinguished;
- answer format;
- uncertainty or missing facts identified; and
- any material error or unsupported conclusion.

If a full timed attempt is impractical, obtain an explicit time estimate and mark it as estimated rather than observed.

## 23–33 minutes — Assisted reviewer-pack test

Show `three_question_reviewer_pack.md`.

Ask the participant to verify the pack against the source documents.

Record:

- verification time;
- material corrections;
- whether any source does not support the associated claim;
- whether the amendment/waiver treatment is clear;
- whether cross-references are sufficient;
- whether missing-information flags help;
- whether the legal-review boundary helps; and
- whether total analyst-plus-reviewer burden appears lower.

## 33–41 minutes — Format and adoption friction

Ask questions 6–9 from `outreach_questions.md`.

Probe:

- direct answer versus clause pack;
- ad hoc answer versus covenant calendar;
- level of detail needed for a decision-ready handoff;
- necessary human-in-the-loop controls;
- confidentiality and system-access constraints; and
- legal or compliance concerns.

## 41–45 minutes — Costly next step

Ask question 10 exactly once.

Classify the response:

- `follow_up_requested`
- `introduction_offered`
- `additional_scenario_requested`
- `sanitized_pilot_discussion`
- `no_next_step`

Do not convert “interesting” or “keep me posted” into a next step.

## Immediately after the session

1. Complete `session_evidence_template.md`.
2. Add one row to the local `evidence_log.csv`, initialized from `evidence_log_template.csv`.
3. Separate observation from inference.
4. Record disconfirming and stop evidence prominently.
5. Delete any accidentally captured identifying or confidential information.
6. Do not change the frozen decision criteria.
