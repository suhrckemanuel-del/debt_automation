# Participant Invitation

Use or adapt this message when inviting a debt-capital practitioner or reviewer. Do not imply that a production product exists.

## Email version

**Subject:** 45-minute review of a debt-document workflow

Hi [first name],

I am testing a narrow workflow question: whether a source-backed reviewer pack can make it faster and safer to establish the current contractual position across a facility agreement, amendment, and waiver.

Would you be willing to join a 45-minute workflow-review session?

The session uses only a small synthetic document set. I will not ask you to share real documents, deal terms, employer information, or any other non-public material. This is an early experiment rather than a product demonstration or sales call.

During the session, I will ask you to:

1. describe a recent comparable workflow in generic terms;
2. work through three questions using synthetic documents;
3. review a source-backed answer pack; and
4. give candid feedback on trust, time, review burden, and whether the workflow is worth pursuing.

If this is relevant to your work, would [two concrete time options] suit you?

Thanks,

Manuel

## Short message

I’m testing whether a source-backed reviewer pack improves a recurring debt-document workflow across agreements, amendments, and waivers. It is a 45-minute review using synthetic documents only—no real deals, documents, or non-public terms. Would you be open to working through three questions and giving candid feedback on trust and total review burden?

## Follow-up

Just following up on the synthetic debt-document workflow review below. It is not a product pitch: the aim is to learn whether the workflow is painful and recurring enough to justify building. If it is not relevant, a direct “not a priority” is useful evidence too.

## Recruitment notes

Record only:

- invitation date;
- broad role category;
- response: accepted | declined | no response;
- scheduled session date, if accepted; and
- whether the person appears to prepare, review, or do both.

Do not store employer names or infer demand from acceptance alone.
