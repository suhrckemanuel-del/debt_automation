# Ten Workflow-Review Questions

Use these questions during recruitment or the session. Ask for sanitized descriptions only. Do not request employer, borrower, lender, transaction, or document names.

1. Think of the most recent time you had to establish the current position across a facility agreement, amendment, waiver, or related finance document. What were you trying to answer?
2. How often does a comparable task arise, and what usually triggers it?
3. What steps and tools do you use today, from receiving the question to handing the answer to a reviewer?
4. Which part consumes the most analyst time, and which part consumes the most reviewer time?
5. What errors or omissions are you most concerned about: missing an amendment, following a cross-reference incorrectly, citing weak support, overlooking missing facts, or something else?
6. Looking at these three synthetic questions, which—if any—resembles a real recurring pain in your work? What does not map?
7. Do the exact citations, currentness notes, and missing-information flags make the output easier to trust and verify? Which element helps or adds work?
8. The third answer refuses to give a final yes/no and requires legal review. Does that caution improve the handoff, or make the output less useful?
9. Would a direct answer, clause pack, covenant calendar, checklist, or another format fit your workflow better? Why?
10. Is there a concrete next step you would take—such as reviewing another scenario, inviting a colleague or reviewer, or discussing a governance-approved sanitized pilot? If not, what prevents that?

## Interview discipline

- Ask about recent behaviour before hypothetical preferences.
- Follow claims with “Can you walk me through the last example?”
- Record exact evidence, not an optimistic interpretation.
- Do not sell past objections.
- Do not count a polite compliment as a next step.
