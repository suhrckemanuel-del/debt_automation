## 1. What is the current maximum LTV, considering amendments or waivers?

### Answer

The original maximum LTV was **65.0%**. Amendment Letter No. 1 replaces that threshold with **70.0% for Test Dates from 15 May through 31 December 2026**.

For the Test Date falling on **30 June 2026 only**, the Lender has waived a Default arising solely from LTV above 70.0% but not above 72.0%, subject to the waiver conditions. The waiver expressly says it does **not** amend the 70.0% threshold.

Therefore, as of 2 July 2026:

- the current contractual LTV threshold is **70.0%**;
- limited waiver relief applies up to **72.0% only for the 30 June 2026 Test Date**; and
- the waiver relief should not be described as a new 72.0% covenant threshold.

## 2. Are distributions restricted, and where are Permitted Distributions defined?

### Answer

Yes. Clause 13.1 prohibits Distributions unless they qualify as a **Permitted Distribution**. That term is defined in **Clause 1.1** and requires, among other things:

- the Distribution to be made from Available Cash;
- no continuing or resulting Default;
- post-Distribution LTV not exceeding 60.0% using the most recent Valuation; and
- a compliance statement delivered at least five Business Days before the proposed Distribution.

There is also a temporary, stricter condition in the Waiver Letter: the Borrower must make **no Distribution from 1 July through 30 September 2026** as a condition of the limited waiver.

### Support status

Supported.

### Sources

1. **Synthetic Facility Agreement — Facility A**, Clause 13.1, page 19:

   > The Borrower shall not make a Distribution unless that Distribution is a Permitted Distribution.

2. **Synthetic Facility Agreement — Facility A**, Clause 1.1, page 3:

   > **Permitted Distribution** means a Distribution made from Available Cash where:
   >
   > (a) no Default is continuing or would result from the Distribution;
   >
   > (b) the Loan-to-Value Ratio, calculated immediately after the Distribution using the most recent Valuation, does not exceed 60.0%; and
   >
   > (c) the Borrower has delivered to the Lender a compliance statement showing the calculation at least five Business Days before the proposed Distribution.

3. **Synthetic Waiver Letter — Facility A**, Section 3(a), page 2:

   > the Borrower making no Distribution from the Effective Date to and including 30 September 2026;

### Currentness note

The Waiver Letter creates an additional temporary restriction relevant on the evaluation date. It does not replace the underlying definition of Permitted Distribution.

### Missing information

None for locating and summarising the restriction. Assessing a proposed Distribution would require the transaction date, amount, source of cash, current Default status, current debt, most recent Valuation, resulting LTV, and evidence of the compliance statement.

### Human review

No legal interpretation requested; this answer identifies the restriction, definition, and temporary waiver condition.

## 3. Can the borrower sell part of the asset and distribute the proceeds?

### Answer

No final yes/no conclusion is provided.

The documents contain several provisions that must be reviewed together:

- A Disposal requires either qualification as a Permitted Disposal or prior written Lender consent.
- Permitted Disposal status depends on arm's-length terms, fair market value, the EUR 1,000,000 aggregate proceeds limit, and no continuing Default, unless the Lender consents.
- Net Disposal Proceeds from a Disposal other than a Permitted Disposal must be applied in mandatory prepayment.
- A Distribution must qualify as a Permitted Distribution.
- The current Waiver Letter additionally requires no Distribution through 30 September 2026.

These extracted provisions do not establish whether the proposed sale satisfies the definitions, whether consent exists, how the proceeds must be applied in the specific circumstances, or whether a later Distribution would be permitted.
