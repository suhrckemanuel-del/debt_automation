# Contrarian Pre-Mortem

**Status:** Pre-session hypotheses; not evidence
**Question:** Why might this agreement-intelligence wedge not deserve to be built?

## No-build case

| Objection | Current evidence status | Evidence that would weaken the objection |
|---|---|---|
| The problem is too infrequent; it occurs mainly during rare financings or amendments. | Untested | At least three participants describe recent, recurring examples rather than exceptional transactions. |
| Existing document search, DMS tools, lawyers, or general AI already solve the job well enough. | Untested | Participants identify specific unresolved currentness, cross-reference, or handoff failures and request another test. |
| The reviewer pack is longer than the underlying work and increases review burden. | Plausible from pack length | Reviewers verify it faster, make fewer material corrections, and prefer it to the current handoff. |
| The useful product is a clause pack or covenant calendar, not an answer assistant. | Plausible adjacent wedge | Participants consistently prefer direct source-backed answers for the tested workflow. Otherwise adapt. |
| Confidentiality, document access, permissions, and system integration dominate the value proposition. | Untested but structurally likely | Participants identify a realistic governance-approved route to a sanitized or controlled pilot. |
| A human still has to read everything, so the tool merely relocates work. | Untested | Combined analyst-plus-reviewer burden falls meaningfully without an increase in material errors. |
| Refusing legal conclusions makes the output feel weak, while giving conclusions would be unsafe. | Core product tension | Reviewers value the missing-fact and legal-review boundary and describe it as a better handoff. |
| The corpus is too clean to predict performance on real agreements. | True | Phase -1 is used only to validate workflow demand; later technical claims depend on a deliberately adversarial benchmark. |

## Strongest alternative explanation for positive feedback

Participants may praise:

- the polish of the artifact;
- the general idea of AI;
- exact citations in principle; or
- the synthetic scenario's clarity,

without having a recurring problem, willingness to change workflow, or ability to provide governed document access.

For that reason, positive comments count weakly unless paired with a recent example, specific workaround, reduced combined burden, or costly next step.

## Cheapest disconfirming test

Run the five serious sessions without building software. Stop or adapt if:

- participants cannot name recent examples;
- current tools are considered sufficient;
- verification burden grows;
- nobody accepts a second session or introduction; or
- confidentiality/legal barriers dominate.

## Pre-session recommendation

**Insufficient evidence.**

The three-question pack is worth testing because it is inexpensive and targets distinct trust problems. No coded prototype is justified yet.
